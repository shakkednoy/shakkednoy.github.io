This page describes how the MIT Social Media Study collects, uses, stores, and protects personal data from people who participate in our research survey. The study was approved by the MIT IRB under protocol number 2506001673.

<strong>Information We Collect</strong>

If you participate, we may collect the following information:

- Your survey responses, including answers about time spent producing content, approximate monthly earnings from content production, and motivations for creating content.

- Your creator account name, username, handle, channel ID, or account URL on TikTok, X/Twitter, or YouTube, if needed to verify eligibility or link your survey response to supplementary account-level information.

- Basic public or permissioned information about your creator account, such as number of followers or subscribers, number of posts or videos, recent posting frequency, public post metadata/metrics, hashtags, and transcripts.

- Recruitment and participation tracking information, such as when an invitation was sent, which recruitment channel was used, whether a survey link was opened, and whether the survey was completed.

- If you participate through Prolific, your Prolific ID.

If we use TikTok, X/Twitter, or YouTube Login as part of the eligibility check, we will request your permission before receiving information from TikTok, X/Twitter, or YouTube. We will not ask for your TikTok, X/Twitter, or YouTube password, post to your account, access private messages, or access contact lists.

<strong>How We Use This Information</strong>

We use this information for academic research on social media content creation. Specifically, we use it to:

- determine whether someone is eligible for the study;

- verify that participants have an active public creator account;

- link survey responses to account-level variables using randomly generated participant IDs;

- study how creators produce content, how much time they spend, how they are compensated, and what motivates them, including how this varies by account size, posting volume, and type of content posted;

- process compensation for eligible completions;

- maintain a record of opt-outs or requests not to be contacted again.

We do not use this information to advertise to participants. We do not sell participant information. We will report only aggregate statistics.

<strong>Lawful Basis</strong>

We rely on your consent to collect and use your social media handle/account identifier and linked account-level information for this study. Participation is voluntary. You may choose not to participate, stop participating, or request that we delete your social media handle/account identifier before it has been deleted according to the retention schedule below.

<strong>How We Store and Protect Personal Data</strong>

Creator usernames, handles, account names, channel IDs, and account URLs are treated as personally identifiable information.

Personal data will be stored on secure MIT Economics-managed servers accessible only to the research team, protected by two-factor authentication, and housed in a secure location.

During the initial linkage period, we store creator account identifiers separately from survey responses and link records using randomly generated participant IDs. Analysis datasets are designed not to contain usernames, account names, handles, channel IDs, or account URLs. Usernames will be deleted once linkage has been completed.

<strong>Retention</strong>

We retain social media handles/account identifiers only as long as needed to verify eligibility, link survey responses to account-level variables, and collect the account-level measures needed for the study. Once we have verified the linkage and collected the supplementary account-level information needed for analysis, we will delete the username/account-name identifiers and retain only randomly assigned participant IDs, survey responses, and derived account-level variables. We expect this to occur within 30 days after the end of data collection for each study wave.

<strong>Third Parties</strong>

If you participate through Prolific, Prolific will process information needed to administer the study and pay compensation according to Prolific's own terms and privacy policy.

If we use TikTok, X/Twitter, or YouTube Login, TikTok, X/Twitter, or YouTube will process the login authorization according to their own terms and privacy policies.

We will not share social media handles, account URLs, individual survey responses, or identifiable study records with advertisers, marketers, or other third parties. We may share de-identified or aggregate research results in academic papers, presentations, or replication materials.

<strong>Your Rights</strong>

You may contact the research team to ask questions about your information, request access to personal data we hold about you, request correction or deletion of your personal data, object to or restrict certain uses of your personal data, or withdraw consent for future use of your social media handle/account identifier.

Because account identifiers are deleted after linkage, we may not be able to identify your individual record after those identifiers have been deleted.

<strong>Public Reporting</strong>

When we write up results from the study, we will describe patterns across groups of creators. We will not name individual creators, publish usernames, handles, account names, or account URLs, or quote individual survey answers in a way that identifies someone.

<strong>MIT Policies</strong>

This study is conducted at MIT and is subject to MIT research oversight and applicable MIT policies on privacy and information security, including MIT's policies on the privacy and disclosure of personal information.
